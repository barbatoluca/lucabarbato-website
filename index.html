01 — About

A brief introduction.

I am a third-year MRes/Ph.D. candidate in Economics and Management at the London School of Economics. I am an applied microeconomist interested in personnel, labour, and organisational economics.

My work uses administrative data, field experiments, and causal inference methods to study how firms design jobs, allocate responsibility, and structure incentives. I collaborate with firms and non-profits on projects and pilots for research and business purposes.

04 — Industry Collaboration

Working with firms and non-profits.

I partner with firms and non-profit organisations on applied research projects — pilots, field experiments, and quantitative studies grounded in operational data. These collaborations inform academic work and, in return, provide partners with evidence on how internal policies shape productivity, retention, and team performance.

If your organisation is considering a rigorous evaluation of a people-practice, incentive scheme, or organisational design change, I would be glad to talk.
